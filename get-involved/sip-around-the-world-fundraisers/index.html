Sip Around the World

Sip Around the World Fundraisers

Sip Around The World fundraising events bring people together for nights of whiskey tasting, auction bidding, and open and honest discussions about men’s mental health.

Hosts of our two biggest fundraising events

is a series of impactful fundraising events supporting ThriveWell, dedicated to raising awareness about men’s mental health and suicide prevention.

Led by Darius Eghdami and Raymond Dich, with invaluable contributions from organizing committee members Martin McNish, Emily-Anne King, Warren Schindler, Matt Sacks, Kevin Shaw, Kevin Murphy, and Kris Alexander, these events have brought communities together for a vital cause.

Featuring inspiring speakers, generous donors, and dedicated volunteers, these events have been instrumental in fundraising, fostering camaraderie, connecting with the community, and building meaningful relationships. They have galvanized friends, family, local businesses, and community members around the vital cause of men’s mental health.

Through their fundraising and support Sip Around the World is a Pioneering Partner of ThriveWell.

Total Funds Raised: $341,500

Thanks to everyone who has come to an event, donated, bid on auction items, played games and, most importantly, listened. Together we are making a difference.

Event Highlights:

  • Sip Around the World II: October 3, 2024
  • Sip Around the World: February 23, 2023

Sip Around the World II

Hosted October 3, 2024, at The Pipe Shop, in North Vancouver.

Sip Around the World II was a special night, once again raising the bar as the highest single fundraising event organized for ThriveWell.

We are incredibly honoured by the generosity and enthusiasm of our community, our sponsors, vendors, volunteers, donors and patrons.

Funds Raised: $200,000

Sip Around the World

Hosted February 23rd, 2023, at The Pipe Shop, in North Vancouver.

It was an incredible night and the biggest single fundraising event ever organized for ThriveWell.

We were incredibly blown away by the support from our community.

Funds Raised: 141,500
